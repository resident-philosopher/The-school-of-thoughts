# School of Thoughts

> School of Thoughts is an open-source education operating system that helps learners develop agency through mission-driven experiments, sprint-based learning, and real-world contribution.

## Why School of Thoughts?

Most educational systems are designed to transmit knowledge.

School of Thoughts is designed to help learners develop agency: the ability to understand themselves, take meaningful action, learn from reality, and contribute to the world around them.

Rather than treating students as passive recipients of information, School of Thoughts treats them as active participants in the development of their own learning.

The goal is not compliance.

The goal is agency.

## The Core Question

School of Thoughts is built around two fundamental questions that guide the design of every mission, experiment, and portfolio within the ecosystem.:

## Internal Value

> What evidence exists that a student sees themselves as valuable?

## External Value

> What evidence exists that reality responds positively to a student’s actions?

Together, these questions form the foundation of meaningful livelihood, contribution, and lifelong learning


## The Six Missions

All School of Thoughts curricula are designed to help learners explore one or more of the following missions:

1. Mission 1 - Who am I and who do I want to be?
2. Mission 2 - How do I become who I want to be?
3. Mission 3 - How do I develop my body to accomplish my goals?
4. Mission 4 - How do I live in harmony with my environment?
5. Mission 5 - How do I design systems that solve problems sustainably?
6. Mission 6 - How do I live in harmony with my community?

## How School of Thoughts Works

School of Thoughts uses a sprint-based learning process inspired by open-source software development and action research.
```
Mission
    ↓
Experiment (Epic)
    ↓
Story Cards
    ↓
Student Pairs
    ↓
Evidence
    ↓
Retrospective
    ↓
Iteration
```
Researchers propose experiments.

Teachers and students break experiments into executable story cards.

Students complete story cards in collaborative sprints through pair-programming.

Evidence is collected throughout the process.

Teachers document observations.

Researchers, teachers, and students learn from the results and improve future versions.

The curriculum evolves through use.

## Open Source Principles

School of Thoughts is built on the belief that educational knowledge should be treated as a commons.

Researchers, teachers, students, and communities are encouraged to:

* Fork repositories
* Run experiments
* Document evidence
* Share findings
* Submit improvements
* Build upon the work of others

Learning is a collaborative endeavor.

The curriculum is never finished.
